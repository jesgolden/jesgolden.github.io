About

Jes Golden, PhD

Philosopher and Jewish meditation teacher.

Jes Golden, PhD, is a philosopher and Jewish meditation teacher. She earned her doctorate at UC Berkeley with a dissertation on gut feelings and embodied epistemology, advised by Alva Noë and John Campbell, with visiting positions at the Institut Jean Nicod (Paris), Harvard, and MIT. She is a Postdoctoral Affiliate at the Meditation Research Program at Harvard Medical School / Massachusetts General Hospital.

Jes teaches meditation regularly through the Institute for Jewish Spirituality (IJS), Or HaLev, and RUACH. She completed the Or HaLev and IJS Gates of Awareness three-year Jewish Meditation Teacher Training and is currently a Senior Teaching Fellow through IJS's Marbeh fellowship.

As a queer Sephardi and Ashkenazi writer, her work on ancestry, practice, and embodied Jewish life both informs her teaching and has appeared in Hey Alma and At The Well. She is currently writing The Body Inherits: Gut Feelings, Ancestry, and the Craft of Knowledge (working title) and developing Merhav: The Wide Place, a digital series on embodied Jewish contemplative life. She lives in Somerville, Massachusetts, with her wife and beloved community.
