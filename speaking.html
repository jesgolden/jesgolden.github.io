Speaking & Workshops

You’re already making decisions with your body. I teach you to do it well.

Tuning into the body requires attuned perception, skillful interpretation, and a critical lens. I teach the theory and practice of how to do all three to help people live more authentically and make better decisions—in keynotes, workshops, multi-session series, and scholar-in-residence weekends.

For Jewish communities & nonprofits Synagogues, JCCs, Hillels, and Jewish organizations.

Reading the body the way we read Torah

Torah teaches us to read with patience and layered attention: we look at the bare meaning, but we also look at surrounding context, we interpret, and look beyond what can be directly perceived with the senses. I teach communities to bring that interpretive posture to the body: to gut feelings, to habitual patterns, and to the kol demamah dakah—the still small voice—expressed in body and in emotion. Each new interpretation—informed by what we know of ourselves, our histories, and the world—creates knowledge we can use to decide when to trust our intuition, always guided by our values.

Signature workshop — What Grows Wild: How Knowledge Is Created in the Body

What does it mean to have embodied knowledge, and how do we tell the difference between wisdom and unwanted old habits? Herbs are plants growing until they are taken into the body, digested, and metabolized: only then do we get to know the nature of their medicine. For those who live in their heads, I propose that getting to know our embodied knowledge is like this. In this workshop, we develop attunement to bodily sensations, responses, and the patterns we all hold in body, heart, and mind. By bringing our attention and Jewish wisdom to our reading of the body, we are better equipped to respond to our bodies with compassion and with wisdom and to avoid making rash decisions based on patterns we no longer endorse.

This workshop includes guided practice, teaching, and discussion on creating knowledge in the body using the interpretive tradition of PaRDeS that Judaism brings to our sacred texts.

For organizations & teams Intuition, discernment, and decision-making.

When to trust your gut—and how to read it

Every leader has heard both "trust your gut" and "don't trust your gut." Both are half right: fast thinking has helped our species survive in moments when we need a snap decision, and that same fast thinking can make mistaken reasoning look like wisdom. Gut feelings are real information, and they are also shaped by history, habit, and bias. I bring a philosopher's training, a background in cognitive science, and a decade of contemplative teaching to workshops on intuition, discernment, and decision-making for teams who want more than a mindfulness app.
