Practice & Community

Let’s practice together.

The body communicates through gut feelings, tension, desire, the pull toward and away. Meditation is one way we can learn to attend to the happenings in the mind, body, and heart. Practicing with a teacher can help cultivate the skills to attend to both pleasant and unpleasant experiences, to discern what we experience in the body-mind. Returning to practice, again and again, can teach us when we should rest in unknowing and when we can derive meaning that can help us shape our lives with wisdom.

Monthly Sits

Segula: A LGBTQ+ Monthly Mindfulness Sit

A closed affinity sit for LGBTQ+ Jews, with the Institute for Jewish Spirituality, hosted by Jes Golden. The Jewish people are sometimes known to Source as a "segula" (Exodus 19:5)—a precious or treasured people; segula also refers to protective amulets and rituals in Jewish folk practices. We welcome our beloved LGBTQ+ Jews to this affinity sit to remember the inherent preciousness of how we express ourselves and relate to the world and to learn with rotating guest teachers each month. In this space, we cultivate the steadiness, groundedness, and strength that can support us in the work of protection, liberation, and authenticity. Please note that this sit is open only to those who are LGBTQ+, whether you've been out for 50 years or whether you are questioning.

Tehom: A Six-Session Series for Deepening Practice

If you've practices a little bit or gone on retreat but struggled to maintain the practice, this is for you. If you are interested in what it looks like to commit to a life of practice—on and off the cushion—come deepen your practice in community. Over six sessions, we will introduce Jewish meditation and its roots in Kabbalah and Hasidism; develop the skills rooted in Jewish and Buddhist tradition for developing compassion and meeting obstacles on the path; and practice seeing and feeling inherent divinity in experience. In small groups, we will deepen our formal practice while beginning to integrate mindfulness and contemplative Jewish wisdom into everyday life.
