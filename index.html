Philosopher · Jewish meditation teacher · Researcher

We create knowledge with our bodies. I teach the theory and the practice.

I'm Jes Golden—philosopher, Jewish meditation teacher, and researcher at Harvard's Meditation Research Program. I teach contemplative practice, rooted in Jewish tradition and grounded in philosophy and contemplative science, as a method for attuning to and interpreting the emotions and wisdom communicated through the body. I show people who live in their heads how to use the body and intuition to find the still, small voice that leads to greater authenticity, clarity, and discernment.

The book

The Body Inherits: Gut Feelings, Ancestry, and the Craft of Knowledge

I'm writing a book (working title above) on how gut feelings carry family, migration, and inheritance—and how Jewish contemplative practice teaches us to tend what we carry and make meaning of it with wisdom.

I teach a workshop on this called What Grows Wild—about accessing and refining what we can know through the body.
